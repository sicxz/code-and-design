## This Week's Focus

Create visual notes from this week's readings on scrollytelling.

You are not learning a tool yet.
You are learning a **pattern**.

Your notes should help you understand how scrolling becomes a storytelling device that controls:

- pacing
- attention
- reveal
- emotion
- meaning

Think of scrollytelling as **choreographing information through space and time.**

## Required Reading (Pick 2)

### Reading 1: Scrollytelling Fundamentals
**Read:** [Webflow — A Guide to Scrollytelling](https://webflow.com/blog/scrollytelling-guide)
**Time:** ~15–20 minutes

**Focus on:**
- What scrollytelling is and is not
- Why scroll is used instead of clicks
- Narrative flow tied to vertical movement
- Common scrollytelling structures

### Reading 2: Editorial and Journalistic Scrollytelling
**Read:** [Shorthand — An Introduction to Scrollytelling](https://shorthand.com/the-craft/an-introduction-to-scrollytelling/index.html)
**Time:** ~10–15 minutes

**Focus on:**
- Scrollytelling in journalism and longform stories
- How text, images, and motion work together
- The role of restraint and pacing
- Reader immersion

### Reading 3: Interactive and Educational Scrollytelling
**Read:** [Genially — What Is Scrollytelling?](https://blog.genially.com/en/scrollytelling/)
**Time:** ~10 minutes

**Focus on:**
- Scrollytelling beyond journalism
- Educational and experiential uses
- Engagement through interaction
- When scrollytelling helps vs. hurts clarity

### Reading 4: No-Code Scrollytelling Patterns
**Read:** [Flourish — No-Code Scrollytelling](https://flourish.studio/blog/no-code-scrollytelling/)
**Time:** ~10 minutes

**Focus on:**
- Scrollytelling as a pattern, not a technology
- Separation of content and motion
- Story-first thinking
- Why tools come last

## Focus Questions

As you take notes, consider these questions:

### Question 1: What Is Scrollytelling Really Doing?

In your own words, define scrollytelling.

**Draw a diagram showing:**
- Scroll position on the Y-axis
- Story progression over time
- How content is revealed or transformed

**Include a metaphor of your own.**
Examples:
- "Scroll as a camera move"
- "Scroll as chapters unfolding"
- "Scroll as a guided walk"

### Question 2: Scroll as Pace Control

How does scrolling control speed and attention?

**Create a visual comparison:**
- Fast scroll experience
- Slow, deliberate scroll experience

**Annotate:**
- When does the user pause?
- When does something new appear?
- What happens if everything animates at once?

### Question 3: Structure Patterns

Identify at least **three scrollytelling patterns** from the readings.

**For each, sketch:**
- Section layout
- What stays fixed
- What moves
- What changes on scroll

**Examples to look for:**
- Sticky elements
- Progressive reveal
- Section-based chapters
- Background transitions

### Question 4: When Scrollytelling Is a Bad Idea

Scrollytelling is not always the answer.

**Create a decision chart:**
- What type of content benefits from scrollytelling?
- What type of content does not?

**Consider:**
- Accessibility
- Cognitive load
- Information density
- User goals

Include at least one cautionary note.
