# Engage: Parallax Dissection

**Points:** 10 (participation)
**Time:** 20 minutes (in-class, Day 1)

---

## TL;DR

Examine scrollytelling sites with a partner. Identify how parallax and scroll-driven animation create depth and pacing. Share one observation with the class.

---

## The Activity

In pairs, open each of these sites on your laptop. **Scroll slowly.** Don't rush — the whole point is noticing what happens as you scroll.

### Sites to Examine

1. [Apple AirPods Pro](https://www.apple.com/airpods-pro/) — Product scroll narrative
2. [The Pudding](https://pudding.cool/) — Pick any visual essay from the homepage
3. [Species in Pieces](http://www.species-in-pieces.com/) — CSS polygon art with scroll reveals
4. [Lusion](https://lusion.co/) — Agency site with bold motion

### For Each Site, Answer Three Questions

**1. LAYERS:** What elements move at different speeds? What stays still while other things move?

**2. TRIGGERS:** Where does animation start? Is it tied to scroll position (moves as you scroll) or just triggered by scroll (plays automatically when you reach it)?

**3. PURPOSE:** Does the parallax/motion serve the content — helping you understand the story — or is it decoration?

### Write It Down

For each site, write one sentence using this format:

> "[Site name] uses [technique] to [effect/purpose]."

Example: "Apple AirPods Pro uses parallax layers to make the product feel like it's floating in space as you scroll through features."

---

## Share Out

Each pair shares **one** observation with the class. Pick your most interesting finding.

---

## Why This Matters

You're about to plan motion for your own scrollytelling site. Seeing how professionals use parallax and scroll-driven animation teaches you what works — and what feels like too much. The best scroll experiences serve the story. The worst ones are just showing off.
