# Engage: Peer Review

**Points:** 10 (Complete/Incomplete)
**Time Estimate:** 30 minutes (in-class)

---

## TL;DR
Swap sites with a partner. Test. Give structured feedback. Receive feedback. Document it.

---

## Submit
1. Submit your completed feedback form for your partner's site (Canvas)
2. Note the 2 changes you'll make based on feedback you received

---

## The Protocol

### Setup (2 minutes)
- Pair up with someone you haven't worked with recently
- Open each other's GitHub Pages URL on YOUR device (not theirs)
- Have the feedback form ready (below)

### Round 1: Review Partner's Site (15 minutes)

Scroll through their site at your own pace. Then fill out the feedback form:

**Feedback Form:**

| Question | Your Answer |
|----------|------------|
| 1. What's the metaphor? Can you explain it in one sentence? | |
| 2. Where did you feel confused or lost? (Be specific — which section?) | |
| 3. Where did you feel delight? What moment made you go "oh, nice"? | |
| 4. Is the scroll pacing comfortable? Too fast? Too slow? Which sections? | |
| 5. Does the pinned section work? What's the effect? | |

**Accessibility Tests:**

| Test | Pass? | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| Tab through the entire site — can you reach all content? | Y/N | |
| Enable Reduce Motion (System Settings → Accessibility) — is all content visible? | Y/N | |
| Is text readable against backgrounds? Any contrast issues? | Y/N | |

**One Thing to Keep:** What's the strongest part of this site?

**One Thing to Change:** What would have the biggest impact if improved?

### Round 2: Swap Roles (15 minutes)

Now your partner reviews YOUR site using the same form.

### Debrief (2 minutes)

Tell your partner:
- The 2 changes you plan to make based on their feedback
- One thing that surprised you about their feedback

---

## Why This Matters

Your site has lived in your head for 4 weeks. You know what every section means because you wrote it. A fresh pair of eyes tells you what ACTUALLY communicates — and what only makes sense to you.

The best work survives contact with an audience.
