# Learn: Scroll as Medium

**Points:** — (not graded, but required before Day 2)
**Time Estimate:** 25 minutes

---

## TL;DR
Watch the ScrollTrigger tutorial. Browse 2 scrollytelling examples and notice how they use scroll.

---

## What to Do

### 1. ScrollTrigger Tutorial (Required — 10 min)
[GSAP ScrollTrigger Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=X7IBa7vZjmo)

Watch for:
- How to add ScrollTrigger to an existing GSAP animation
- What `trigger`, `start`, and `end` do
- What `scrub` feels like vs. trigger-and-play
- How to use markers for debugging

### 2. Browse 2 Scrollytelling Sites (Required — 15 min)

Pick 2 from this list. Scroll through them slowly. Notice:

- **[The Pudding: Film Dialogue](https://pudding.cool/2017/03/film-dialogue/)** — Data visualization driven by scroll
- **[Apple AirPods Pro](https://www.apple.com/airpods-pro/)** — Product story through scroll
- **[How the Virus Got Out (NYT)](https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2020/03/22/world/coronavirus-spread.html)** — News narrative through scroll

**For each site, answer:**
1. Which sections trigger-and-play (animation fires once when you scroll to it)?
2. Which sections scrub (animation progress is linked to scroll position)?
3. Is the scroll pacing comfortable or exhausting?
4. What's the "showpiece" moment — the section that makes you go "whoa"?

---

## The Big Idea

Scroll is not just navigation. In scrollytelling, **scroll is time.** The user controls the pace. Your job is to design the experience so that pace feels natural — not too fast (they miss content), not too slow (they get bored).

Next class, you'll build this into your own site.
