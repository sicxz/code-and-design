## What to Include

Document anything that helps you process and remember the material:

**Variables & Data Types:**
- What's a variable? (use a metaphor that works for you)
- The difference between `let` and `const` — when do you use each?
- Data types: strings, numbers, booleans — draw examples

**Strings:**
- What does "immutable" mean for strings?
- String concatenation — how do you combine text?
- Template literals with `${}` — how are they different?

**Code Clarity:**
- Semicolons — required or optional?
- Comments — `//` vs `/* */`
- Why do we use `console.log()`?

**Personal additions:**
- Questions that came up
- Connections between ideas
- "Aha" moments or things that clicked
- Things you found confusing (bring to class!)

## Focus Questions

As you take notes, consider these questions:

1. **What's the difference between `let` and `const`?** When would you use one over the other?
2. **Why do we use `console.log()`?** What would happen if we couldn't see our output?
3. **When should you use comments?** What makes a comment helpful vs. noise?

---

## Tips for Effective Visual Notes

- **Don't copy text** — rewrite concepts in your own words
- **Draw diagrams** — boxes for variables, arrows for assignments
- **Use visual metaphors** — variables as labeled boxes, strings as chains of characters
- **Include code snippets** — but annotate them with what each part does
- **Leave space** — you'll want to add things during class discussion
- **Mark confusion** — circle or star things you don't fully understand yet
