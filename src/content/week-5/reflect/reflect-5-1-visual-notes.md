# Reflect: Visual Notes — The Metaphor Map

**Points:** 10 (Complete/Incomplete)
**Time Estimate:** 20–30 minutes

---

## TL;DR
Sketch a visual map showing how your JS concepts connect to your metaphor. Not a list — a drawing.

---

## Submit
1. Photo or scan of your visual notes (Canvas upload)
2. Digital version acceptable (Figma, Procreate, iPad sketch)

---

## The Prompt

Draw a map of your metaphor. Show how each JavaScript concept you've learned connects to a chapter in your story.

This is not a bulleted list. It's a **visual** — a diagram, a flowchart, a storyboard, a mind map, an illustrated journey. Use the format that makes the connections clearest.

**Include:**
- Your metaphor's name/title
- At least 5 JS concepts mapped to metaphor elements
- Visual connections showing how concepts relate to each other
- The narrative arc: where does the story start? Where does it end?

**Concepts to map:**
- `console.log()` / DevTools
- Variables (`let`, `const`)
- Events (`addEventListener`)
- Conditionals (`if/else`)
- `classList.toggle()`
- Design tokens / CSS custom properties
- `localStorage`

---

## Why This Matters

You're about to spend 5 weeks building a site around this metaphor. This sketch is your compass. When you're deep in GSAP code in Week 7, you'll look at this and remember what story you're telling — and why.
