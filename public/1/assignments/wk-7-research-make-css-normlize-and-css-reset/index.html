Week 7 — Research & Make CSS Normalize and CSS Reset

Web Design Fundamentals · Spring 2026

Active

Step 1: Historical Foundation

Read Eric Meyer's "Reset Reasoning" (2007)

  • What to notice: The original problem CSS resets solved
  • Note: What browser inconsistencies bothered developers enough to create this?

Step 2: Modern Evolution

Read Josh Comeau's "A Modern CSS Reset"

  • What to notice: What changed between 2007 and now?
  • Note: Why does Josh modify or reject parts of Meyer's approach?

Step 3: Wider Landscape

Look at Jeff Starr's "Killer Collection of CSS Resets" at Perishable Press

  • What to notice: The variety of approaches (aggressive resets vs. gentle normalization)
  • Research task: Find 2-3 other reset/normalize approaches online

Step 4: Critical Questions (Your Notes Should Address)

  • Why reset at all? (What problem does it solve?)
  • Why Josh's version over Eric's? (Be specific about 2-3 differences)
  • How do you actually use these? (Link tag? Import? Inline?)
  • What's a CSS boilerplate? (vs. a reset)
  • Why use a boilerplate? (When does it help/hurt?)
  • Why is Normalize.css different from resets? (Philosophy shift)

Step 5: Deliverable

Build a mini-site (one page) that demonstrates your research:

  • Your notes/thinking visible
  • Answers to the critical questions
  • Links to sources
  • Link this from your main index.html
